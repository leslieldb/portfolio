Goals

Where am I now, and how far can I go?

Where am I now?

Right now, I have just completed my IT Programming program at Nova Scotia Community College, and I am now enrolled in a Bachelor of Computer Science at Dalhousie.

I have been looking for work terms, and just recently someone reached out to me regarding one. This will be my first IT work term ever if I get it, and I'm very eager to start my way into the industry.

Where am I going?

My end educational goal is a Bachelor's Degree of Computer Science at Dalhousie, which I am well on my way towards. This has been a dream of mine for almost a decade now, and I'm very excited to finally get this under way. I have no doubts that I'll do very well.

As for my end career goals, I'm not certain about them yet. What I do know is that after I graduate from Dalhousie, I want to make my way up the software engineering and software development positions, with my current main goal being the senior developer position. I want to be able to gain many advancements throughout my programming career and build my professionalism even more.

How do I get there?

If I want to achieve my career goals, the biggest thing I need to do is start networking. I have many friends in the industry who have told me that this is by far the most important thing when it comes to a career, especially in IT.

The first thing I will do regarding this is build up my LinkedIn. I already have a few connections, but I learned that being active on the site, posting once a day, will turn the algorithm more in your favor.
This can bode very well, as employers browse LinkedIn all the time to recruit people via the hidden job market.

I am an introvert with autism, so networking events aren't really my thing, but I may decide to go to a few of those as well. For now, however, this is definitely my best bet to start reaching my goals.
